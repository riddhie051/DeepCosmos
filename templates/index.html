Dataset Overview

This astronomy dataset contains carefully selected images of celestial objects collected from publicly available astronomical image sources. The dataset was prepared to train a deep learning model capable of recognizing and classifying different categories of space objects.


Total Images: 941 Astronomy Images


Classes Included:

  • 🌠 Constellation – Patterns formed by groups of stars in the night sky.
  • 🌌 Galaxy – Massive systems containing billions of stars, gas and dust.
  • ☁️ Nebula – Interstellar clouds of gas and cosmic dust.
  • 🪐 Planet – Celestial bodies orbiting stars with unique surface features.
  • ⭐ Star – Self-luminous astronomical objects that generate energy through nuclear fusion.

Image Resolution: All images were resized to 224 × 224 pixels before training.


Dataset Split: 80% Training Data and 20% Validation Data.


Preprocessing & Augmentation: Image normalization, horizontal flipping, rotation, zooming and augmentation techniques were applied to improve model generalization and reduce overfitting.


Challenges: Certain classes such as Galaxy and Nebula share similar visual characteristics, making classification more challenging. Variations in brightness, color distribution and object scale also increase the complexity of the task.
